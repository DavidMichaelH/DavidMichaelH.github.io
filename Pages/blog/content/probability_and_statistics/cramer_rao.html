Cramér-Rao Bound

Theorem

Let \(\hat{\theta}\) be an unbiased estimator of \(\theta\). Then \[ \operatorname{Var}(\hat{\theta}) \geq \frac{1}{I(\theta)}, \] where \(I(\theta)\) is the Fisher information.

Proof

Let \[ L(\theta) = f(X|\theta) = \prod_{i=1}^n f(x_i|\theta) \] be the likelihood function. Let \(\ell(\theta) = \log L(\theta) \) and \(\ell_i(\theta) = \log f(x_i|\theta)\). be the log-likelihood functions. Since \(\hat{\theta}\) is unbiased, we have \[ 0 = \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} (\hat{\theta} - \theta) \, f(X|\theta) \, dX. \] Differentiating both sides with respect to \(\theta\) (and interchanging the derivative and the integral) gives \[ 0 = \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} (\hat{\theta} - \theta) \, f(X|\theta) \, dX. \] This becomes \[ 0 = \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} (\hat{\theta} - \theta) \, f(X|\theta) \, \frac{\partial \ell(\theta)}{\partial \theta} \, dX - 1. \] Applying the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality then gives \[ 1 \leq \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} (\hat{\theta} - \theta)^2 f(X|\theta) \, dX \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} \left( \frac{\partial \ell(\theta)}{\partial \theta} \right)^2 f(X|\theta) \, dX. \]

Notice that \[ \frac{\partial \ell(\theta)}{\partial \theta} = \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \sum_{i=1}^n \ell_i(\theta) = \sum_{i=1}^n \frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \ell_i(\theta). \] Also, since \[ \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{\partial}{\partial \theta} \ell_i(\theta)\right] = 0, \] it follows that

\[ \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} \left( \frac{\partial \ell(\theta)}{\partial \theta} \right)^2 f(X|\theta) \, dX = \sum_{i=1}^n \int_{\mathcal{X}} \left( \frac{\partial \ell_i(\theta)}{\partial \theta} \right)^2 f(x_i|\theta) \, dx_i = I(\theta). \]
\[ \int_{\mathcal{X}^n} \left( \frac{\partial \ell(\theta)}{\partial \theta} \right)^2 f(X|\theta) \, dX \] \[ = \sum_{i=1}^n \int_{\mathcal{X}} \left( \frac{\partial \ell_i(\theta)}{\partial \theta} \right)^2 f(x_i|\theta) \, dx_i = I(\theta). \]

Therefore, we obtain \[ \operatorname{Var}(\hat{\theta}) \geq \frac{1}{I(\theta)}, \] which is the desired result.
