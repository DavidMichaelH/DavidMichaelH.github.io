Likelihood Ratio Achieving Testing Risk

In binary hypothesis testing, one aims to minimize the sum of Type I and Type II errors. Under the setting where data are drawn from one of two distributions \(P\) and \(Q\), and assuming that these measures are absolutely continuous with respect to a common reference measure, it turns out that the likelihood ratio test achieves the minimax risk of \[ 1 - TV(P,Q), \] where \(TV(P,Q)\) denotes the total variation distance between \(P\) and \(Q\).

Theorem

Consider a hypothesis testing problem given by \[ H_0 : X \sim P, \] \[ H_1 : X \sim Q, \] where \(P\) and \(Q\) are probability measures on \(\mathcal{X}\) that are absolutely continuous with respect to the measure \[ \mu = \frac{P+Q}{2}. \] Then, the likelihood ratio test is minimax optimal.

Proof

Let \(\mu = \dfrac{P+Q}{2}\) and \(p=\dfrac{dP}{d\mu}\) and \(q=\dfrac{dQ}{d\mu}\). Then the likelihood ratio can be given by $$ A = \{ x : p(x) \geq q(x) \} .$$ \[ P(A^c) + Q(A) = \int \textbf{1}_{p < q} d P + \int \textbf{1}_{p \geq q} d Q \] \[ \int \textbf{1}_{p < q} p d\mu+ \int \textbf{1}_{p \geq q} q d\mu = \int p \wedge q d \mu \] Using the identity $$ \min(a,b) = \dfrac{a+b-|a-b|}{2}$$ we have that \[ \int p \wedge q d \mu = \int \dfrac{p+q-|p-q|}{2} d \mu = \int \dfrac{p+q}{2} d \mu - \int \dfrac{|p-q|}{2} d \mu \] \[ = 1 - TV(P,Q) .\] As we stated, this is the minimax lower bound for this testing problem.
