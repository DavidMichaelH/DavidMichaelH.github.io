Properties of f-Divergences: Non-Negativity and Equality Condition

Theorem

Let \(P\) and \(Q\) be probability measures on \(\mathcal{X}\) that are both absolutely continuous with respect to a measure \(\mu\). Let \(f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}\) be a convex function satisfying \(f(1)=0\). Then the \(f\)-divergence between \(P\) and \(Q\) is defined as

\[ D_f(P\|Q) = \mathbb{E}_Q\left[ f\left( \frac{p(X)}{q(X)} \right) \right] = \int f\left( \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \right) q(x) \, d\mu(x). \]
\[ D_f(P\|Q) = \mathbb{E}_Q\left[ f\left( \frac{p(X)}{q(X)} \right) \right] \] \[ = \int f\left( \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \right) q(x) \, d\mu(x). \]
The \(f\)-divergence is non-negative, and moreover, if \(f\) is strictly convex then \(D_f(P\|Q)=0\) if and only if \(P = Q\).

Proof

The claim that \(D_f(P\|Q)\) is non-negative follows immediately from Jensen's inequality. Indeed, since \(f\) is convex, we have \[ \begin{aligned} D_f(P\|Q) &= \int f\left( \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \right) q(x) \, d\mu(x) \\ &\geq f\left( \int \frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\, q(x) \, d\mu(x) \right) \\ &= f(1) = 0. \end{aligned} \]

Now, suppose that \[ D_f(P\|Q) = \mathbb{E}_Q\left[ f\left( \frac{p(X)}{q(X)} \right) \right] = 0. \] Then, by another result on strict convexity and Jensen's inequality, it must be that \[ \frac{p(X)}{q(X)} = \mathbb{E}_Q\left[\frac{p(X)}{q(X)}\right] = 1 \quad \text{almost surely}, \] which implies that \(p(x)=q(x)\) almost everywhere, and hence \(P = Q\).
