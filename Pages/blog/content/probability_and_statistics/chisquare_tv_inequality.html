Chi-squared to Total Variation Inequality

Theorem

Let \(P\) and \(Q\) be probability measures on \(\mathcal{X}\) that are both absolutely continuous with respect to a measure \(\mu\). \[ TV(P,Q) \leq \dfrac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \chi^2(P||Q)} . \]

Proof

Indeed \[ TV(P,Q) = \dfrac{1}{2}\mathbb{E}_Q\left | \frac{p(X)}{q(X)} - 1 \right | \] \[ \leq \dfrac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \mathbb{E}_Q\left( \frac{p(X)}{q(X)} - 1 \right)^2 } = \dfrac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \chi^2(P||Q) }. \]
