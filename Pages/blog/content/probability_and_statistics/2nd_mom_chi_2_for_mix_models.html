\(\chi^2\) Formula For Mixture Models

Theorem

Define the mixture model \(P_{\pi} = \int P_{\theta} d\pi(\theta)\) \[ \chi( P_{\pi} |Q) = \mathbb{E} G(\theta,\theta') - 1 \] where \[ G(\theta,\theta') = \int_X \dfrac{dP_{\theta}}{dQ} \dfrac{dP_{\theta'}}{dQ} dQ \]

Proof

\[ \chi( P_{\pi} |Q) = \int_X \left (\dfrac{dP_{\pi}}{dQ} \right)^2 dQ -1 \] \[ = \int_X \left (\int \dfrac{ d P_{\theta} }{dQ} d\pi(\theta) \right)^2 dQ -1 \] \[ = \int_X \int_{\Theta} \int_{\Theta} \dfrac{ d P_{\theta} }{dQ} \dfrac{ d P_{\theta'} }{dQ} d\pi(\theta) d\pi(\theta') dQ -1 \] \[ = \int_{\Theta} \int_{\Theta} \left ( \int_X \dfrac{ d P_{\theta} }{dQ} \dfrac{ d P_{\theta'} }{dQ} dQ \right ) d\pi(\theta) d\pi(\theta') -1 \] \[ = \mathbb{E} G(\theta,\theta') - 1 \]
